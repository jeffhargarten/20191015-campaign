How Klobuchar's presidential campaign fares, by the numbers

By Jeff Hargarten
Star Tribune October 16, 2019

Since Sen. Amy Klobuchar's (D, Minn.) snowy Feb. 10, 2019 presidential annoucement, she's so far survived a crowded field of Democratic primary candidates, qualifying for DNC debates at least until year's end.

Headed into 2020 though, she ranks in the race's lower rungs, her poll numbers, fundraising and other metrics not resembling those of a potential party nominee.

Here's Klobuchar's campaign by the numbers so far:

Polling average

Klobuchar's has never quite reached even the middle tier of candidates, and as of mid-October 2019, her RCP polling average is stuck near the bottom of the pack.

1.3%
ranked 9th place

Individual donors

Individual small donors are a key factor to making the DNC debate stage, and though Klobuchar qualifies through November, a significant portion of her campaign's financial support draws from Minnesotans, which doesn't expand the map compared to other candidates.

79,000
ranked 11th place

Campaign fundraising

Klobuchar's fundraising lags considerably behind the primary's frontrunners, and in the first quarter of 2019 a third of her small donations were confined to Minnesota. Her second quarter FEC filing showed a drop from the $9 million previously raised by the campaign.

$12.6 million
ranked 8th place

Political endorsements

Klobuchar has secured campaign endorsements from high-profile Minnesota politicians – including the governor, four DFL U.S. representatives and former Vice President Walter Mondale – but hasn't yet received key endorsements from those outside the state.

12
ranked 5th place

Average debate time

As the DNC debate stage has shrunken and consolidated since the summer, Klobuchar has gradually made herself heard more often – though her usual debate talk time is still two minutes less than average and lags behind frontrunners like former Vice President Joe Biden (D, Conn.), Sen. Bernie Sanders (I, Vt.) and Sen. Elizabeth Warren (D, Mass.).

10 min.
ranked 9th place

Average Google search share

Search interest for candidates ebbs and flows, and Klobuchar saw her greatest spike in Google searches after the third debate. Overall though, daily searches from January to September show she's lagged far behind other candidates.

2%
ranked 13th place
